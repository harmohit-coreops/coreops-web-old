Selection of right data migration platforms, methodologies and best practices

Change is the only thing constant in the fast paced digital environment today. Be it the upgrade of your Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP), the transition to a new Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform or the necessary transition to the cloud, there is one key process that it all relies on is data migration.

Data migration refers to the transfer of data, between and outside a storage system, format, or location and its successful implementation is vital in ensuring that contemporary businesses achieve success.

But the process of moving the petabytes of business-critical data is much more complicated than just a simple copy and paste. The selection of the appropriate data migration platforms, processes, and compliance with best practices are between a smooth transition and an expensive system crash.

Let’s take you through the platforms and strategic and important practices. We will also demonstrate how a cohesive platform, such as DataCORE, can streamline this whole process, even the most complex migrations being easy to handle.

The reason why selecting appropriate data migration platforms is important

The risks associated with not thinking about appropriate planning and choice of enterprise data migration platforms are enormous. The consequences of poor data migration can be devastating and include:

Prolonged downtime: Delays in introducing new systems, affecting revenue and productivity directly.
Data loss and corruption: Irreversible loss or destruction of valuable customer, financial, or operational data.
Compliance problems: Neglecting regulatory compliance during transfer, resulting in fines.

On the other hand, proper investment in the correct enterprise data migration platforms will reap benefits such as:

Speed and efficiency.
Assurance of data quality.
Strong governance of the entire project.
Common data migration platforms

The available platforms for data migration differ significantly depending on the complexity and scale of the project.

Automated assistants

These are automated platforms that guide users step-by-step. Ideal for smaller and simpler migrations, especially when coding knowledge is limited. They minimize human error and speed up simple tasks. For example, Microsoft offers migration assistants with guided interfaces.

Platform-specific platforms

Designed for a single operating system or environment. For example, a company using Apple devices may rely on specialized Mac data migration platforms. These provide smoother integrations and handle proprietary file systems effectively.

Enterprise-level solutions

Used for highly scaled systems such as ERPs, large databases, or complex CRM environments. These platforms support mixed environments (cloud and on-premises). Dedicated CRM data migration platforms maintain customer relationship and sales history during migrations. Solutions like DataCORE enable scalability, security, and pre-constructed connectors for ERP, CRM, and hybrid cloud migrations.

ERP systems methods of data migration

Finding the appropriate method is as critical as the platform, especially in mission-critical systems like ERP.

Methods of ERP data migration
Method	Description	What to use when
Big-bang	All data is transferred simultaneously and the new system goes live immediately.	Suitable for simple systems that can be scheduled offline.
Phased	Data and business functions are transferred in stages or modules.	Best for large, complex ERP systems where continuity matters.
Unspecialized	Manual typing or copy-pasting of small amounts of data.	Limited to non-critical, small-scale data.
Automated	Specialized platforms handle enterprise data transfer and conversion.	Necessary for large, high-integrity projects.

Challenges: Ensuring data integrity, minimizing downtime, and training users. Platforms like DataCORE simplify ERP migrations with automated validation and pre-built connectors.

Major comparisons to know before making a choice
Data migration vs Data integration
Data migration: A one-time process of moving data from an old system to a new system (like house moving). Often includes transformation and cleansing.
Data integration: A continuous process ensuring data is synchronized and available in multiple systems in real time (like connecting utilities).
Data transfer vs Data migration
Data transfer: Simple copying of data without altering format or structure.
Data migration: A structured process involving ETL (Extract, Transform, Load), ensuring compatibility with the new system and maintaining data quality.
Optimal data migration best practices

To ensure success:

Pre-migration planning and governance: Establish governance, security, and stakeholder buy-in.
Data cleansing and profiling: Clean and standardize data before migration (“Garbage in, garbage out”).
Mapping of data: Create a blueprint mapping each source field to target fields.
Test migration runs: Perform trial runs to identify issues and train users.
Post-migration audit and monitoring: Verify accuracy and set up real-time monitoring.
